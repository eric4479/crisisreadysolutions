Why AHA-Aligned Training Matters

Evidence-Based, Science-Driven Emergency Response Training

Crisis Ready Solutions is committed to providing CPR and First Aid training that follows American Heart Association (AHA) guidelines. But why does this matter? Learn how AHA-aligned training gives you the highest confidence that you're learning the most effective, evidence-based techniques to save lives.

The Science Behind AHA Training

Evidence-Based Science

The American Heart Association updates its CPR and ECC guidelines every five years based on the latest scientific research and clinical data. AHA training ensures you're learning techniques proven to improve survival rates in real emergencies.

Impact: Survival rates increase by up to 30-40% when bystanders perform CPR following AHA guidelines.

Globally Recognized

AHA certification is honored worldwide. Whether traveling, working internationally, or changing employers, your AHA CPR certification proves you've met the highest standards recognized across 150+ countries.

Benefit: Your certification opens doors in healthcare, aviation, education, and corporate settings globally.

Standardized Protocols

AHA guidelines provide consistent, standardized protocols that medical professionals follow in hospitals and emergency response teams. Learning these same techniques creates alignment between public bystander response and professional emergency care.

Advantage: Seamless handoff to emergency medical professionals when they arrive.

Workplace Compliance

OSHA and most employer safety requirements specifically require or recommend AHA-certified CPR and First Aid training. AHA certification meets legal and regulatory standards for workplace safety mandates.

Requirement: Many employers legally require AHA certification for employment or liability protection.

Key Facts About AHA Training

Since 1974

The American Heart Association has provided CPR training for over 50 years, continuously improving techniques based on the latest science.

30-40% Survival Improvement

Studies show that immediate CPR following AHA guidelines increases survival rates from sudden cardiac arrest by 30-40%.

Time is Critical

AHA guidelines emphasize early CPR and early defibrillation. Brain damage can occur within 4-6 minutes without oxygen.

Hands-Only CPR

AHA research shows hands-only CPR for adults is as effective as traditional CPR with rescue breaths, removing barriers to bystander action.
